Vaccine Court Awarded Millions To Two Children With Autism. So What Changed?

Friday, May 1, 2015 13:39

(Before It's News)

 

As the debate rages on about finding a link between autism and vaccines, we need only to look back a short couple of years in history to find where Pharmaceutical companies, the medical associations and the Government have admitted to wrong doing. In this particular case in 2013, their admission came with a payout that soared into the millions of dollars.

The federal Vaccine Injury Compensation Program, or “vaccine court,” awarded millions of dollars under the name of pain and suffering, as well as caring for the needs of two children for lifelong injuries, to two kids who were found to have autism after being vaccinated.

The Government, though, didn’t want to admit guilt (likely a part of the huge settlement). And the case facts weren’t disclosed (they were hidden). This was one item in a long list of admissions by the Government. The Government, via this case and others, all but admits that vaccines cause encephalopathy, which produces a permanent injury and creates symptoms of autism (not a full admission, but the facts are what they are).

 

The court case meant relatives of all walks be deposed and speaking to the idea that the child was normal prior to receiving a vaccine. One case involved 10 year old Ryan Mojabi. His parents gave him the full slate of vaccinations and “MMR,” and they have testified that this caused “severe and debilitating injury to his brain, described as Autism Spectrum Disorder.” The parents have spoke much regarding the case.

Another claim: “as a cumulative result of his receipt of each and every vaccination between March 25, 2003 and February 22, 2005, Ryan has suffered . . . neuroimmunologically mediated dysfunctions in the form of asthma and ASD.”

According to the docket, Ryan’s case was transferred to Vaccine Court in 2009 where the Government admitted that MMR had cause the encephalopathy.

So then what gives today? What’s changed?

Well for one, the pro vaccination camp has gotten strong in terms of marketing and using distractions, for example the case of Disney, to build and garner support. A campaign of fear mongering has caused most to feel afraid of anti-vaccination stances. Social campaigns have shamed mothers who choose not to vaccinate and that alienation has led to lots of mothers turning back to vaccination.

Another issue has been the involvement of Hollywood celebrities. Jenny McCarthy’s somewhat crazy approach followed by revisionist “I never said that” stance harmed the cause. She was a bad representative for a cause of such huge magnitude. Then you add in Jimmy Kimmel and Penn and Teller, and the waves of vaccination began to curl and break with greater social size.

But history never changes, it always stays the same. And unless someone can go back in time to solve why admissions such as the aforementioned example occurred, how could it be possible to trust our Government in any of these cases? The key is your own research and your own study.
